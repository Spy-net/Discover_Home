Historic Bihar
Land of Enlightenment

Bihar, a state in eastern India, is a rich tapestry of culture, heritage, and history. Nestled along the Gangetic plains, Bihar boasts a unique blend of tradition and modernity. With a history dating back to ancient times, Bihar has been a cradle of knowledge and learning, being home to the renowned Nalanda University, an ancient center of education. The state's cultural fabric is intricately woven with festivals like Chhath Puja, a celebration dedicated to the Sun God, and the vibrant folk dances like Jat-Jatin and Jhijhian.

In recent years, Bihar has seen a resurgence in economic and infrastructural development, showcasing a blend of its glorious past and a promising future. Bihar's cultural richness and resilience make it a captivating destination for those seeking a glimpse into India's diverse heritage.

Royal Jaipur
India's Pink City

Jaipur, known as the "Pink City" and the capital of Rajasthan, is a splendid destination that seamlessly blends history, culture, and architectural marvels, creating a tapestry of vibrant experiences for travelers. Nestled within the folds of ancient times, Jaipur stands as a testament to India's royal heritage and artistic finesse. The city is adorned with palaces, forts, and bustling markets, offering a journey back in time.

The Pink City's architectural wonders, including the Hawa Mahal, City Palace, and Amer Fort, narrate tales of valor and grandeur. The intricate carvings, colorful facades, and regal structures transport visitors to an era of Maharajas and opulence. The City Palace, with its fusion of Rajput and Mughal architecture, stands as a symbol of Jaipur's rich history.

Sacred Uttarakhand
Rishikesh and Haridwar – Where Spirituality Flows

Rishikesh and Haridwar, nestled in the breathtaking state of Uttarakhand, stand as twin cities along the sacred banks of the Ganges River, offering a spiritual and cultural haven for seekers and travelers. These cities are not just places; they are gateways to the divine, embodying the essence of spirituality and natural beauty.

Rishikesh, often referred to as the "Yoga Capital of the World," is a sanctuary for those seeking inner peace and holistic well-being. The city is adorned with ashrams and yoga retreats, attracting spiritual enthusiasts and yogis from around the globe. The iconic Laxman Jhula and Ram Jhula, suspended bridges over the Ganges, add to the city's tranquil charm.

Haridwar, known for its vibrant ghats and the grandeur of Ganga Aarti, is a city that reverberates with religious fervor. The Har Ki Pauri ghat, where the sacred Ganges leaves the mountains and enters the plains, is a revered spot for pilgrims. The nightly Ganga Aarti, a spectacular ritual of lights and devotional songs, creates a mesmerizing ambiance along the river.

Taj Mahal - Aagra's Crow
Out of 7 Wonders

Agra, a city steeped in history and grandeur, is home to one of the world's most iconic symbols of love - the magnificent Taj Mahal. Situated in the northern state of Uttar Pradesh, Agra stands as a testament to India's rich cultural heritage and architectural brilliance.

Agra Fort, another UNESCO World Heritage Site, stands as a testament to the grandeur of the Mughal Empire. The fort's red sandstone walls enclose a complex of palaces, gardens, and audience halls, offering a glimpse into India's regal past.

Beyond its historical landmarks, Agra boasts a rich tapestry of culture and tradition. The bustling markets, such as Kinari Bazaar and Sadar Bazaar, showcase exquisite handicrafts, jewelry, and traditional textiles.

Celebrate With Us

As you travel through the country, be a part of our festivals and feasts

Holi

Holi is a popular and significant Hindu festival celebrated as the Festival of Colours, Love, and Spring. Additionally, the day signifies the triumph of good over evil, victory of Vishnu as Narasimha over Hiranyakashipu

Chat Puja

Chhath is an ancient Hindu festival native to the Indian subcontinent, more specifically, the Indian states of Bihar, Uttar Pradesh, Jharkhand and the Nepalese Autonomous provinces of Koshi, Madhesh and Lumbini.

Chat Puja

This is a major Hindu festival dedicated to the worship of Goddess Durga. It usually spans over nine days and involves elaborate rituals, cultural performances, and the immersion of idols in water bodies.

Diwali

Diwali is the Hindu festival of lights, with variations celebrated in other Indian religions. It symbolises the spiritual "victory of light over darkness, good over evil, and knowledge over ignorance".

Navratri

This is a nine-night Hindu festival dedicated to the worship of Goddess Durga in her various forms. It involves fasting, prayer, dance forms such as Garba and Dandiya Raas, popular in Gujarat and other parts of India.

Ganesh Chaturthi

Ganesh Chaturthi celebrates Lord Ganesha's birth, the elephant-headed deity, revered as remover of obstacles and god of wisdom. It involves the installation of Ganesha idols in homes, and idol immersion in water bodies.

Janmashtami

Also known as Krishna Janmashtami celebrates Lord Krishna's birth in Hinduism. Fasting, devotional songs, and reenactments of Krishna's childhood antics Dahi Handi ceremonies mark this joyous occasion of his childhood antics.

Dussehra

Vijayadashami symbolizes victory of good over evil, commemorating Lord Rama's triumph over Ravana. Celebrated with Ravana effigy burnings, Ramleela, and processions. Dussehra" means "ten" (Dasha) and "defeat" (Hara) in Sanskrit

Travel Information

Best Time To visit India

The best time to visit India for holidays depends on the type of experience you seek. The tourism season in India varies across regions due to its diverse climate. The peak tourism season in India starts from September to June.



For wildlife enthusiasts, winter is ideal as national parks are open, and wildlife sightings are abundant. Hill stations are most enjoyable during summer to escape the scorching heat of the plains. For trekking and hiking adventures, winter, monsoon, autumn, and spring offer pleasant weather. Plan your trip according to your preferences, and you'll discover India's incredible beauty and cultural richness, making it an unforgettable holiday destination. September to April can be considered an ideal time to explore India for tourism holidays.

How to get around in India?

India's transportation network facilitates seamless travel experiences for tourists worldwide. With extensive road connectivity, travelers can easily explore different regions. The rail network offers scenic and comfortable journeys to various destinations. Major cities boast efficient metro systems for hassle-free urban transportation.



Domestic and international airports, located across the country, provide direct flights from major cities globally, ensuring convenient access. Whether you're exploring historical sites in Delhi, serene backwaters in Kerala, majestic palaces in Rajasthan, or scenic landscapes in Himachal Pradesh, India offers diverse transportation options. From buses and cabs to auto-rickshaws, various modes cater to different budgets, making travel accessible and enjoyable.

Must-Follow Travel Tips for Exploring India

When visiting India, it's essential to check out important travel tips to ensure a smooth and enjoyable trip. Follow recommended guidelines for packing, cultural respect, local transportation, and staying safe. These tips will help you make the most of your India tour and have a memorable experience.



  • Research and Plan Ahead: Familiarize yourself with India's culture, climate, and popular destinations. Plan your itinerary well in advance to make the most of your trip.
  • Obtain Necessary Visas: Apply for the appropriate visa type and ensure your passport is valid for at least six months beyond your travel dates.
  • Pack Wisely: Pack lightweight, breathable clothing suitable for the weather. Don't forget essentials like sunscreen, a hat, comfortable footwear, and insect repellent.
  • Respect Local Customs: India is culturally diverse, so be respectful of local customs, traditions, and dress codes, especially when visiting religious places.
  • Stay Hydrated: Carry a water bottle and stay hydrated, especially during hot weather. Use bottled or boiled water for drinking and brushing your teeth.
  • Eat Wisely: Enjoy the delicious Indian cuisine but be cautious with street food. Choose restaurants with good hygiene practices to avoid foodborne illnesses.
  • Negotiate Prices: Bargaining is common in markets, so negotiate prices for souvenirs and services to get the best deal.
  • Stay Safe: Be vigilant in crowded places and watch out for pickpockets. Use reputable transportation services and avoid traveling alone at night.
  • Use Local Transport: Experience India's culture up close by using local transport like trains, rickshaws, and buses.
  • Respect Wildlife: If visiting wildlife sanctuaries, maintain a safe distance from animals and follow park rules for a responsible and enjoyable experience.
  • Stay Connected: Get a local SIM card or international roaming plan to stay connected with family and access maps and travel apps.
  • Carry Cash: While credit cards are widely accepted in cities, have some cash on hand for smaller establishments and remote areas.
  • Mind the Monsoon: Be prepared for heavy rainfall during the monsoon season, and plan outdoor activities accordingly.
  • Respect Photography Rules: Always seek permission before taking photos of people or religious sites, as some may be sensitive to photography.
  • Embrace the Chaos: India can be bustling and chaotic, but embrace the vibrant atmosphere, and you'll be rewarded with a truly unforgettable travel experience.
